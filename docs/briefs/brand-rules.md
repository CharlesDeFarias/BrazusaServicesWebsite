# Brazusa Brand Rules

Prescriptive rules for all copy, design, and build work on the Brazusa site.
When these conflict with generic website best practices, these win.
Read this before writing copy, designing sections, or making layout decisions.

---

## Positioning

Core sentence:
Brazusa is a locally owned, professionally run cleaning operations partner for Boston
rentals and workplaces, built for clients who need dependable execution without having
to chase the vendor.

Backup formulations (use when the core sentence is too long for context):
- Dependable cleaning, structured like an operation.
- Stable teams, clear accountability, less oversight.
- Cleaning built for operators, not just occupants.
- Service you do not have to manage.

---

## Decision Rules

**1.** If a section could appear unchanged on 50 other local cleaning websites, it is wrong.
Rewrite until it is specific to what Brazusa actually does differently.

**2.** Operational credibility wins over warm branding language.
Friendly tone is fine. Vague tone is not.

**3.** Lead with reduction of management burden.
Every section must answer at least one of:
- How do I know the work actually gets done?
- How do standards hold over time?
- What happens if something goes wrong?
- Why won't I have to keep following up?

**4.** Do not flatten buyer segments into one pitch.
STR operators, offices, and clinics share some trust logic but buy for different reasons.
Segment-specific language is not optional.

**5.** Identity supports credibility. It does not carry it.
Locally owned and immigrant-owned can appear, but always inside a professionalism frame.
Never ask for emotional trust when operational trust can be earned instead.

**6.** The site should look and behave like a business that has standards.
Visual impression: order, process, calm confidence.
Not: trying hard to look impressive.

**7.** No filler reassurance. Every section must do at least one of:
- differentiate Brazusa
- reduce perceived risk
- explain how service actually works
- segment the buyer correctly
- move toward conversion

## What Each Buyer Fears (what the site must not miss)

**STR operators:**
- bad reviews or refunds from unready units
- emergency cleaner scrambles
- guests arriving to a unit that was not cleaned
- having to monitor every turnover themselves

**Offices:**
- inconsistent crews and recurring misses
- complaints from staff
- communication black holes
- random labor instead of managed service

**Clinics:**
- sloppy routines in sensitive environments
- vendors making unsafe assumptions
- "we clean everything" claims with no evidence of process
- poor professionalism in patient-facing spaces

**Property managers:**
- vendor inconsistency across locations
- slow issue handling
- spending their own time coordinating details the vendor should own

## Copy Rules

**Avoid these words and phrases in Brazusa copy:**

reliable, high quality, professional (as a standalone claim), flexible, affordable,
eco-friendly, satisfaction guaranteed, attention to detail, peace of mind,
seamless experience, trusted name, healthier environment, customized solutions,
we care about your space, your satisfaction is our priority, exceptional service

These describe intention, not mechanism. They do not lower buyer uncertainty.

**Prefer mechanism-based language:**

- built for / structured for / managed through
- completed and confirmed
- one accountable point of contact
- clear service standards
- fewer follow-ups, fewer surprises
- less day-to-day oversight
- consistent recurring execution
- issues flagged before they become problems
- locally owned, professionally run
- visible process / documented standards

**Writing test:**
Weak: Reliable service you can count on.
Better: Clear standards, consistent execution, and communication you do not have to chase.

If a sentence describes intention, rewrite it to describe mechanism.

## Claim Safety

The following claims require operational verification before appearing in production copy.
Tag unverified instances with [VERIFY] rather than shipping them.

- inspections on every service
- verified completion / photo proof
- defined response-time commitments
- dedicated account manager per account
- backup coverage if primary team is unavailable
- same-day issue handling
- low staff turnover
- consistently used checklists
- clinic-specific protocols
- turnover-specific guarantees
- supply / linen / reset handling
- direct-hire (vs subcontractor) workforce

If a section depends on one of these claims to work, it needs verification first,
not copy that quietly implies it.
